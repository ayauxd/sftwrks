# Softworks Trading Company: Complete Copywriting Overhaul

## Strategic Foundation

### Target Audience Profile

**Who they are:**
- Business owners, executives, practice owners (law firms, medical practices, agencies, retailers)
- Age 45-65, established, successful in their domain
- Revenue: $500K - $20M annually
- Team size: 5-50 people
- Budget: $10K-$100K for the right solution
- NOT technical—they hire people for that
- Time-poor, decision-fatigued, skeptical of trends

**Their current AI situation:**
- Hearing about AI constantly, feeling pressure
- Confused about what's real vs. hype
- Worried about being left behind AND about wasting money
- Don't know who to trust
- Don't have time to figure it out themselves
- Had bad experiences with past "tech transformations"

**What makes them buy:**
- Trust in a person, not a product
- Clear, measurable outcomes (hours saved, dollars saved)
- Low risk / easy exit if it doesn't work
- Feeling understood, not lectured to
- Seeing people like them succeed

**What pushes them away:**
- Jargon, acronyms, tech-speak
- Feeling stupid or talked down to
- High-pressure sales tactics
- Vague promises without specifics
- Having to manage another complex vendor relationship

---

## The Core Reframe

### Current Positioning Problem

The site currently speaks like a **tech consultant** trying to impress **tech-savvy buyers**.

It uses language like:
- "AI Strategy & Integration Advisory"
- "Vision-Language Models"
- "GraphRAG approach"
- "Constitutional AI wrapper"
- "Token economics"

This works for CTOs and technical founders. It **alienates** the high-ticket, low-tech buyer.

### New Positioning

**From:** "We're AI experts who can implement sophisticated solutions"

**To:** "We're the people who make AI actually work for businesses like yours"

**Core message:** AI is just a tool. Like any tool, it needs someone who knows how to use it. That's us.

### New Tagline Options

1. "AI that works. No PhD required."
2. "We make AI make sense."
3. "Your business, with AI that actually helps."
4. "AI without the headache."
5. "Finally, AI that works like it should."

**Recommended:** "AI that works. No PhD required."

## Voice Guidelines Summary

| Do | Don't |
|-----|-------|
| Use "you" and "your" | Use "we" and "our" as subject |
| Short sentences | Long, complex sentences |
| Plain English | Jargon, acronyms |
| Specific outcomes | Vague benefits |
| Acknowledge concerns | Dismiss objections |
| Be warm but direct | Be either cold or fluffy |
| Use analogies | Assume technical knowledge |

**Reading level target:** 8th grade or below
**Sentence length target:** Under 20 words average
**Tone:** Knowledgeable friend who happens to be an expert
